A useful first answer

Start with what you need to know.

Every matter is different. These are the questions that usually open the conversation.

Do I need a will or a trust?

That depends on your assets, family, goals, and the level of control you want during incapacity and after death. The right plan may use a will, one or more trusts, beneficiary designations, or a combination.

How long does probate take in Florida?

Timing varies with the estate, creditor period, tax issues, court requirements, disputes, and the work needed to identify and distribute assets. An early review can identify the likely path and avoid preventable delay.

When is guardianship necessary?

A court may appoint a guardian when a person cannot make some or all decisions and less restrictive alternatives are not sufficient. The court process is designed to address both protection and individual rights.

Can you help if I live outside Florida?

Yes. The firm handles Florida probate, trust, property, and planning matters for clients, fiduciaries, families, and attorneys located outside the state.

What should I bring to a first consultation?

Bring the documents you already have and a short list of the people, assets, deadlines, or concerns involved. If something is missing, that is part of what the first conversation can clarify.
