Ask Jill: Why Jill Chose Guardianship

Guardianship sits in a different place from planning work. The crisis is real, the intervention is real, and the result is visible.

Most attorneys who handle elder law gravitate toward planning. Jill went toward guardianship.

Elder law is a broad category. Guardianship is its own world, with its own statutory framework, court rules, procedural rhythm, and subset of practitioners.

The ward is in a safer place. The bleeding stopped. The exploitation ended. The family fight resolved.

That is the pull of the work. Guardianship is one of the few areas where the result of the intervention can be seen directly. The work is hard, clients are often in crisis, and the court schedules are unforgiving. But it is also one of the places where finishing a case means knowing exactly what was accomplished.
