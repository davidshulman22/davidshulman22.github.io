How Long Does Probate Take in Florida?

The most common probate question has a frustrating answer: a formal administration almost never finishes in six weeks.

The timeline is driven by law, not just by how fast anyone works.

The Timeline

Getting Appointed

The petition is filed, the court appoints the personal representative, and Letters of Administration issue. Until Letters issue, nobody has authority to handle estate assets.

The Creditor Window

Once notice to creditors is published, creditors have three months to file claims. This is the floor under every Florida formal administration.

Active Administration

Assets are marshaled, the inventory is filed, claims are paid or contested, taxes are handled, and the estate moves toward distribution.

Closing

Final accounting or waivers, receipts, distributions, and discharge usually add another phase at the end.

What Makes Probate Slow

  • No original will
  • Family conflict
  • A house that must sell
  • Tax complications
  • Unresponsive beneficiaries
