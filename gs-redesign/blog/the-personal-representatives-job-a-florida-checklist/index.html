The Personal Representative's Job: A Florida Checklist

You have been named personal representative of a Florida estate. Here is what the job involves and what not to touch too early.

The personal representative is the person the will, or the court, puts in charge of the estate.

Before Appointment

  • Deposit the original will with the clerk of court.
  • Secure the property and keep insurance in force.
  • Order death certificates.
  • Do not touch the money before Letters of Administration issue.

After Letters Issue

  • Get an EIN and open an estate bank account.
  • Marshal accounts, real estate, vehicles, business interests, and tangible property.
  • Publish notice to creditors and serve known creditors.
  • File the inventory, evaluate claims, handle taxes, distribute, and close.

The Two Rules

Never mix estate funds with your own, and never distribute early. Those are the fiduciary mistakes that turn a family administration into personal liability.
