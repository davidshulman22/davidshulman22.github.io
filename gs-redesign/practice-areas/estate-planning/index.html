Estate Planning

Florida estate planning that accounts for homestead, incapacity, asset titling, beneficiary designations, tax concerns, and the people who will have to live with the plan.

Estate planning decides who controls your finances if you cannot, who makes medical decisions for you, and how your assets transfer at death.

What a Florida Plan Should Cover

A complete plan usually includes a will or revocable trust, durable power of attorney, designation of health care surrogate, and living will. More complex plans may include irrevocable trusts, special needs provisions, planning for minor children, or business succession structures.

Florida Homestead

Florida homestead rules can override what a will says, especially in second marriages or when minor children are involved. Titling the residence correctly is often the difference between a plan that works and one that creates an avoidable court proceeding.

When a Trust Makes Sense

A revocable trust can avoid probate when it is properly funded. If the trust is signed but assets are never moved into it, the family may still need probate.
