Florida Estate Administration

Probate

Guidance for personal representatives and families through formal administration, summary administration, creditor claims, homestead issues, and closing the estate.

Probate is the court process that moves assets out of a deceased person's name when there is no automatic transfer mechanism.

Formal vs. Summary Administration

Summary administration is the shorter process for smaller estates or estates where the decedent has been dead for more than two years. Formal administration is the full process, with a personal representative, creditor period, inventory, claims, distributions, and discharge.

How Long It Takes

Formal administration usually cannot be completed quickly because creditors receive a statutory claim window. Straightforward estates often take months; contested or complicated estates take longer.

What the Personal Representative Does

  • Deposit the original will
  • Get appointed and open an estate account
  • Marshal and inventory assets
  • Publish and serve notice to creditors
  • Evaluate and pay valid claims
  • Distribute assets and close the estate

The two practical rules: do not mix estate money with personal money, and do not distribute before the creditor picture is clear.
